# Customer Journey Map for IdeaWeb

## 1. Aware
- **Trigger Event**: Non-technical founder realizes the need for an online presence to validate their business idea.
- **Friction Points**: Overwhelmed by technical jargon and complexity of existing website builders; fear of failure in creating a professional-looking site.
- **User Emotions**: Confusion, anxiety, hope for a solution.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Clear, engaging marketing materials that simplify the website building process; testimonials from other non-technical founders who succeeded using IdeaWeb.
- **Metric**: Awareness conversion rate (percentage of target audience that visits the website or engages with marketing content).

## 2. Consider
- **Trigger Event**: Researching options for website builders; comparing features and ease of use.
- **Friction Points**: Difficulty in understanding the differences between various no-code tools; concern about hidden costs or limitations.
- **User Emotions**: Curiosity, skepticism, cautious optimism.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Interactive demos or webinars showcasing the ease of use; transparent pricing with no hidden fees; comparison charts highlighting IdeaWeb's advantages.
- **Metric**: Consideration rate (percentage of users who engage with product demos or sign up for newsletters).

## 3. Try
- **Trigger Event**: User signs up for a free trial or demo of IdeaWeb.
- **Friction Points**: Initial setup may feel daunting; potential technical issues during the trial period; lack of guidance or support.
- **User Emotions**: Excitement mixed with apprehension; desire for reassurance.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Onboarding tutorials that guide users step-by-step; responsive customer support during the trial; community forums for peer support.
- **Metric**: Trial conversion rate (percentage of users who convert from trial to paid subscription).

## 4. Adopt
- **Trigger Event**: User decides to purchase a subscription after a successful trial experience.
- **Friction Points**: Concerns about ongoing costs; fear of commitment to a platform that may not meet future needs.
- **User Emotions**: Relief, satisfaction, lingering doubt.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Personalized follow-up communication; offering a loyalty discount for early adopters; showcasing success stories of other users who have scaled their websites.
- **Metric**: Adoption rate (percentage of trial users who convert to paid users).

## 5. Expand
- **Trigger Event**: User seeks additional features or tools to enhance their website as their business grows.
- **Friction Points**: Complexity in understanding advanced features; potential overwhelm with too many options.
- **User Emotions**: Ambition, excitement, potential frustration.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Tailored recommendations based on user behavior; easy-to-follow guides for advanced features; proactive outreach for upselling additional services.
- **Metric**: Expansion rate (percentage of existing customers who upgrade their plans or purchase additional features).
