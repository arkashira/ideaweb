# marketing-plan.md

## Positioning
**Primary Positioning Statement:**  
"Empower non-technical founders to launch their ideas effortlessly with IdeaWeb, the no-code website builder designed for speed and simplicity."

**Alternative Positioning Statements:**
1. "Transform your vision into a live website in minutes with IdeaWeb, the no-code solution for aspiring entrepreneurs."
2. "Launch your startup without the tech hassle using IdeaWeb, the intuitive website builder for non-technical founders."
3. "IdeaWeb: Your partner in turning concepts into websites, no coding required."

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
1. **Persona 1: Sarah the Startup Founder**  
   - **Daily Job:** Managing business operations, pitching to investors, and networking.  
   - **Budget:** $500/month for tools and services.  

2. **Persona 2: Mark the Creative Freelancer**  
   - **Daily Job:** Delivering projects to clients, managing social media, and seeking new clients.  
   - **Budget:** $300/month for tools and services.  

3. **Persona 3: Jessica the Non-Profit Director**  
   - **Daily Job:** Overseeing programs, fundraising, and community outreach.  
   - **Budget:** $200/month for tools and services.  

## Channels
1. **Content Marketing**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $50 per acquired customer through blog posts, tutorials, and case studies.  

2. **Social Media Advertising (LinkedIn and Facebook)**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $100 per acquired customer through targeted ads aimed at non-technical founders.  

3. **Partnerships with Startup Incubators**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $75 per acquired customer through referral programs and workshops.  

## Content Cadence
- **Week 1:** Launch blog post introducing IdeaWeb, social media teasers.  
- **Week 2:** Publish a tutorial on building a website in under 30 minutes, share on social media.  
- **Week 3:** Case study featuring a successful launch using IdeaWeb, promote via email newsletter.  
- **Week 4:** Host a webinar on "No-Code Solutions for Founders," promote through social channels.  
- **Week 5:** Share user-generated content and testimonials, engage with audience on social media.  
- **Week 6:** Publish an article on the benefits of no-code tools for startups, share across platforms.  
- **Week 7:** Create a video demo of IdeaWeb, distribute via YouTube and social media.  
- **Week 8:** Launch a limited-time promotion for early adopters, announce via email and social media.  
- **Week 9:** Publish a blog post on common mistakes in website building and how to avoid them.  
- **Week 10:** Share insights from user feedback and product updates, engage community on social media.  
- **Week 11:** Host a Q&A session on social media, addressing common questions about IdeaWeb.  
- **Week 12:** Recap of the journey so far and a call to action for new users to join IdeaWeb.

## Launch Milestones
- **D-30:** Finalize product features and user interface; begin beta testing with select users.  
- **D-0:** Official launch of IdeaWeb; initiate marketing campaigns across all channels.  
- **D+30:** Collect user feedback, analyze initial metrics, and adjust marketing strategies as needed.  
- **D+90:** Review overall performance, assess customer satisfaction, and plan for future product enhancements.

## Success Metrics
- **Daily Active Users (DAU):** Target 500 DAUs by D+90.  
- **Monthly Recurring Revenue ($MRR):** Target $10,000 MRR by D+90.
